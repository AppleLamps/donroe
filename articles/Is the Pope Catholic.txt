The question used to be the punchline of the English language. “Will it rain in Seattle?” Is the Pope Catholic? “Does fire burn?” Is the Pope Catholic? The phrase worked because the answer was the most self-evident yes the language could produce.

It is no longer self-evident. In twelve months of public teaching, Pope Leo XIV has contradicted the Nicene Creed his own missal prints, the First Commandment his own Catechism enforces, the exclusivity of Christ his own predecessors anathematized other religions for denying, and the just war tradition his own Church has guarded for sixteen hundred years. This article is the cross-examination of whether the word “catholic” still applies to the man who holds the Catholic office.

I will take the word by its own historic definition... use that definition as the measuring stick... lay every public action of Pope Leo XIV alongside it... and let the evidence speak.

I. The Measure
You cannot measure a man unless you know what you are measuring against.

Catholicism, as the Roman Catholic Church itself defined it for centuries, was a content. A body of doctrine. A confession with edges sharp enough to cut.

That confession is still printed in every Catholic missal in the world. The Nicene Creed. “I believe in one Lord Jesus Christ, the only-begotten Son of God... God from God, Light from Light, true God from true God... begotten, not made, consubstantial with the Father.” Every Catholic prays this every Sunday. Every Catholic priest is required to teach it. Every council of the Church for sixteen centuries has guarded it.

The Creed draws a line. On one side stands the confession that Jesus is “true God from true God.” On the other side stands every system that denies it. Islam denies it. Buddhism denies it. Hinduism denies it. Judaism denies it. The Creed exists to make that denial explicit and to mark its rejection of the Son as a rejection of the Father.

The First Commandment is the foundation underneath the Creed. “I am the Lord your God... You shall have no other gods before Me” (Exodus 20:2-3). The Catholic Catechism, in section 2084, cites this commandment and reaffirms... “It is the first commandment that condemns polytheism.” Condemns. Not engages with. Not dialogues alongside.

Scripture closes the door on negotiation. John 14:6... “I am the way, the truth, and the life. No one comes to the Father except through Me.” Acts 4:12... “Nor is there salvation in any other, for there is no other name under heaven given among men by which we must be saved.” Galatians 1:8... “Even if we, or an angel from heaven, preach any other gospel to you than what we have preached to you, let him be accursed.”

Now bring in the Catholic Church’s own historic voice. Not for our authority. For its self-incrimination.

Pope Pius IX, in his 1864 Syllabus of Errors, condemned the proposition... “Man may, in the observance of any religion whatever, find the way of eternal salvation, and arrive at eternal salvation” (Proposition 16). Condemned. As error. As the proposition forbidden to Catholics.

Vatican I, in Pastor Aeternus (1870), defined the role of the Pope with surgical precision... “The Holy Spirit was promised to the successors of Peter not so that they might, by His revelation, make known some new doctrine, but that, by His assistance, they might religiously guard and faithfully expound the revelation or deposit of faith transmitted by the apostles.”

By his own institution’s most solemn definition, the Pope’s job is guardianship. He hands on what he received. He does not innovate. He does not develop. He does not dialogue the deposit into something new. He guards it.

That is the measuring stick. The Nicene Creed. The First Commandment. John 14:6. Acts 4:12. Galatians 1:8. The Syllabus of Errors. Pastor Aeternus. The unbroken chain of magisterial pronouncement that Catholicism, before everything else, is the confession that Jesus Christ is the only Way and that no other path leads home.

Every measurement that follows is taken against that stick.

II. The Record
Now lay Pope Leo XIV’s pontificate alongside it.

The Vesak Message (May 12, 2025)

Four days after Pope Leo XIV’s election, the Vatican’s Dicastery for Interreligious Dialogue, operating under his direct authority, issued a public message to the Buddhist world for the festival of Vesak. The document was titled “Buddhists and Christians in Liberating Dialogue for Our Time.”

The message contained this passage, published by the Vatican on the Vatican’s own website...

“The Buddha taught that, ‘he who is free from craving and attachment, is perfect... he indeed is the bearer of his final body. He is truly called the profoundly wise one, the great man’ (Dhammapada, Ch. 24, V. 352). For Jesus, knowledge of Truth is liberating: ‘You will know the truth, and the truth will make you free’ (John 8:32).”

Read the construction. The Buddha taught X. For Jesus, the parallel teaching is Y. Two teachers. Two paths. Listed side by side. Quoted side by side. Joined in the same paragraph as if their teachings were two routes up the same mountain.

The Buddha is a mortal philosopher who was born around 563 BC. He died. He stayed dead. He preached the cessation of desire as a path to liberation from rebirth. He never claimed to be God. His followers do not claim he is God.

Jesus Christ is the eternal Son of God who took on flesh, was crucified for sin, rose bodily from the grave, and now sits at the right hand of the Father. John 8:32, the verse the Vatican quoted, sits inside a chapter where Jesus also says... “Before Abraham was, I AM” (John 8:58). The Jews picked up stones to kill Him for that statement because they understood it as a claim to deity. Jesus did not retract it.

The Vatican placed these two figures in literary parallelism, taking the chapter that ends with Jews trying to stone Jesus for claiming to be God and softening it into a teaching about “shared pursuit of truth” alongside a man who never claimed to be God at all.

This is the institution whose Syllabus of Errors condemned the proposition that any religion can lead to eternal life. This is the office whose own Catechism, in section 841, calls Christ “the one mediator between God and men” (1 Timothy 2:5).

His own Vatican apparatus published a document that contradicted both.

The Algiers Mosque (April 13, 2026)

I documented this in the previous article. The summary is unchanged. Pope Leo XIV entered the third-largest mosque on earth, removed his shoes, walked through the prayer hall, stood in silent meditation before the mihrab pointing toward Mecca, signed the visitor’s book, called the building “the space that is of God,” and described the worshippers there as people seeking “the presence of the Most High.”

A mosque exists to deny that Jesus is the Son of God. The shahada recited inside it confesses Allah as the only God and Muhammad as his messenger... a confession that excludes Christ by definition. The mihrab the Pope stood before is the architectural symbol of a religion built on the explicit theological rejection of Christ’s deity.

The question every honest Catholic has to face is whether Christians and Muslims worship the same God. The Quran answers it explicitly. Surah 4:171 forbids the Christian doctrine of the Trinity. Surah 4:157 denies the crucifixion. Surah 112 denies the Sonship... “He neither begets nor is born.” The God of Islam is defined by the rejection of the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit. By calling the mosque “the space that is of God,” the Pope endorsed worship inside it as worship of the same God his own Creed says Islam denies. The two propositions cannot stand together.

The Nostra Aetate Anniversary (October 28, 2025)

The 60th anniversary of Nostra Aetate was celebrated in two parts. The first was an interfaith prayer service at the Roman Colosseum, organized by the Community of Sant’Egidio, with approximately 300 representatives of world religions present. The second was an evening event in the Paul VI Audience Hall at the Vatican.

The Pope’s address was published on the official Vatican website. He opened with these words... “Sixty years ago, a seed of hope for interreligious dialogue was planted. Today, your presence bears witness that this seed has grown into a mighty tree.”

He referenced the document’s claim that the Catholic Church “rejects nothing of what is true and holy” in non-Christian religions, and quoted Nostra Aetate‘s line that other religions “reflect a ray of that truth which enlightens all men.”

Then he cited Pope Francis’s 2024 statement at an interfaith meeting in Singapore... “God is for everyone, and therefore, we are all God’s children.”

We are all God’s children. Universally. By default. Without faith in the Son.

John 1:12 says the opposite, in plain English... “But as many as received Him, to them He gave the right to become children of God, to those who believe in His name.”

The right to become a child of God is given to those who receive Christ. Pope Leo XIV approvingly cited a teaching that says the right is universal. Two statements. One from the Apostle John. One from the chair of Peter. They cannot both be true.

The image is concrete. The Pope of Rome stood in front of the Colosseum, where Christians were once fed to lions for refusing to burn incense to false gods, and lit candles in shared prayer with the religious heirs of those very systems... Hindus, Jainists, Shintoists, representatives of African Traditional Religions, all gathered for collective worship of “the one divine Mystery that embraces all creation.”

The Christians who died on that ground would not have recognized the ceremony.

The James Martin Audience (September 1, 2025)

Pope Leo XIV granted a 30-minute private audience to Father James Martin, the Jesuit priest who runs Outreach, the most prominent LGBTQ advocacy ministry inside the Catholic Church. Martin reported afterward that the Pope’s message was “Todos, todos, todos.” Everyone, everyone, everyone.

The Catechism of the Catholic Church, section 2357, calls homosexual acts “intrinsically disordered” and “contrary to the natural law.” It cites Genesis 19, Romans 1, 1 Corinthians 6, and 1 Timothy 1 as the scriptural basis.

The Pope hosted the teaching’s loudest internal opponent without retracting a syllable of it. The press walked away reporting that the audience confirmed a posture of “openness and welcome.” The doctrine survives on paper while its weight in practice quietly drops to zero. That is the modernist’s preferred kill. The text remains. The signal sent to 1.3 billion Catholics is that the text no longer matters.

The Press Conference on Sexual Morality (April 23, 2026)

On the return flight from Africa, Pope Leo XIV was asked about Cardinal Reinhard Marx of Germany, who had moved to bless same-sex couples in defiance of Vatican policy. The Pope’s answer is a masterpiece of subversion-by-affirmation...

“I think it’s very important to understand that the unity or division of the Church should not revolve around sexual matters. We tend to think that when the Church is talking about morality, that the only issue of morality is sexual. And in reality, I believe there are much greater and more important issues, such as justice, the equality, freedom of men and women...”

He held the formal line. He said the Holy See does not agree with formalized blessings of same-sex couples. Then he immediately stated that sexual morality is not where the unity of the Church should rest.

The Catechism, the Council of Trent, and two thousand years of Catholic moral theology say sexual morality is constitutive of holiness because the body itself is theological. Genesis 1 establishes male and female as the image of God. Christ’s first miracle is at a wedding. The marriage of the Lamb is the consummation of Revelation. Catholic theology treats sexuality as one of the central canvases on which the image of God is displayed.

The Pope said it should not divide the Church. The Catechism says it is part of the unchanging moral law. One of these is binding. Both cannot be.

The Just War Rejection (April 2026)

In multiple statements during the spring of 2026, Pope Leo XIV declared... “God is never on the side of those who wield the sword.” He claimed Jesus “does not listen to the prayers of those who wage war, but rejects them.” He called for an “unarmed peace.”

I documented in the previous article what the Bible itself says. Exodus 15:3. Joshua 10:11. 1 Samuel 15. Psalm 144:1. Hebrews 11:32-34. Revelation 19. The God of the Bible is a warrior who personally trains hands for battle and returns at the end of the age on a war horse drenched in blood.

Catholic moral theology does not need the Old Testament to refute the Pope here. It has its own thousand-year tradition. Augustine, the very saint whose religious order Pope Leo XIV belongs to. Aquinas, in the Summa Theologiae (II-II, Q. 40), articulating the conditions of just war. The Catechism, sections 2308–2310, affirming the right of legitimate defense by military force. The U.S. Conference of Catholic Bishops issuing a public statement after Vice President Vance’s rebuke acknowledging that Catholic teaching permits “self-defense, once all peace efforts have failed.”

The Pope contradicted the Catechism. The Pope contradicted Augustine. The Pope contradicted Aquinas. The Pope contradicted the entire just war tradition his own Church has guarded for sixteen hundred years.

III. The Defense... Tested
A serious case requires hearing the strongest version of the opposing argument. Pope Leo XIV has defenders. Their argument deserves to be presented at full strength before being weighed.

Defense One... Nostra Aetate

The 1965 Vatican II declaration explicitly states that the Catholic Church “rejects nothing of what is true and holy” in non-Christian religions. Defenders argue that the Pope’s interfaith gestures are simply the lived application of a binding magisterial document. He is not innovating. He is implementing.

Defense Two... The “Seeds of Truth”

Justin Martyr, writing in the second century, taught that the logos spermatikos... the seed of the Word... was scattered throughout pagan philosophy. Wherever truth appeared, it pointed back to Christ. Defenders cite this Patristic concept as the basis for treating Buddhist or Muslim or Hindu teachings as partial reflections of the one Word who became flesh.

Defense Three... Pastoral Gradualism

The argument runs that the Pope cannot demand of a Muslim in Algiers, a Buddhist in Bangkok, or a homosexual Catholic in Berlin the full weight of doctrine in a single encounter. He must meet people where they are. He must build bridges. Doctrine is preserved formally. Pastoral practice is what changes.

Defense Four... “Conviction, Not Compromise”

The Pope’s own phrase from the Nostra Aetate anniversary. Dialogue, he said, “begins not in compromise, but in conviction.” Defenders argue this means he is not surrendering Catholic truth. He is engaging the world from a position of confident faith.

Now the counter-examination.

Against Defense One

Nostra Aetate is a document from 1965. The Syllabus of Errors is from 1864. Vatican I is from 1870. The Catechism cites both as authoritative. By the Catholic Church’s own teaching on the development of doctrine, no later magisterial statement may contradict an earlier one. If Nostra Aetate is read in such a way that it permits the equation of Christ with Buddha, the equation of mosques with sacred space, and the universal childhood of God apart from faith in the Son, then Nostra Aetate has been weaponized against the very tradition it claimed to develop.

The defenders’ use of Nostra Aetate requires throwing out Pius IX, Vatican I, and the unbroken pre-1965 magisterium. That is rupture, not development.

Against Defense Two

Justin Martyr did teach the logos spermatikos. He also wrote his First Apology to the Roman emperor explicitly to convert him. Justin used the seeds-of-truth concept as a wedge for evangelism. He died as a martyr because he refused to burn a single grain of incense to the gods of Rome.

The early Fathers’ theology of partial truth in pagan thought always functioned as a bridge into Christ. Paul on Mars Hill (Acts 17) is the canonical model. He found an altar to the unknown god, used it as a hook, and then preached the resurrection and called for repentance. He did not light candles with the Athenians. He told them God now “commands all men everywhere to repent” (Acts 17:30).

The defenders have inverted Justin Martyr. They have turned a missionary tool into a shield against missionary urgency. The seed-bearer would not recognize what they have built with his concept.

Against Defense Three

The pastoral gradualism argument has been used to justify every doctrinal collapse in the modern Church. The argument assumes the people in the pews and on the streets are too fragile to hear the gospel directly. The Apostles operated by the opposite confidence. Peter at Pentecost preached Christ crucified to a crowd of Jews who had just participated in His execution and three thousand were saved that day (Acts 2). Stephen preached the truth knowing it would get him stoned, and it did (Acts 7). Paul preached in synagogues until they threw him out of cities.

Christ Himself met the Samaritan woman at the well (John 4) and refused interfaith dialogue. He told her... “You worship what you do not know; we know what we worship, for salvation is of the Jews.” He named her sin. He offered her living water. The encounter ended with her telling her village... “Come, see a Man who told me all things that I ever did. Could this be the Christ?”

That is the model of pastoral encounter Christ left. It is direct. It names need. It offers Himself as the answer.

Against Defense Four

“Conviction, not compromise” is a beautiful phrase. It is also a measuring stick. Conviction in the absence of proclamation collapses into branding.

The Pope had the largest Christian platform on earth in Algiers, in Cameroon, at the Colosseum, on the return flight from Africa. He did not name Jesus Christ as the only way of salvation in a single one of those settings. He did not call any non-Christian present to repent. He did not present the gospel.

Conviction that never costs you anything is sentiment. The Apostles’ conviction cost them their lives because they would not soften the exclusivity of Christ. The current Pope’s “conviction” wins him standing ovations from the European Parliament.

The defenses fail under the weight of the Catholic Church’s own historical claims. The defender of Pope Leo XIV is forced into the position of saying that Pius IX in 1864 condemned the very thing his successors are now teaching, that Justin Martyr would have applauded the divorce of his concept from evangelism, and that Christ at the well demonstrates a model He would have rejected by Algiers.

These are positions that dismantle Catholicism while wearing Catholic vestments.

IV. The Verdict
I come back to the question.

Is the Pope Catholic?

If “Catholic” means baptized and validly ordained and elected by the College of Cardinals... yes. By the institutional mechanics. By the legal procedure. By the visible office.

If “Catholic” means what the institution itself has claimed for two thousand years... a confession of the Nicene Creed, a defense of the First Commandment, the proclamation of Christ as the only way of salvation, the guarding of the deposit of faith handed down by the Apostles, the rejection of religious indifferentism, the maintenance of the moral law against the spirit of the age... then the evidence on the page says he is not.

He is something the Catholic Church for sixteen centuries had a name for. Modernist.

The Catholic Church coined that label and defined its anatomy with terrifying precision in 1907. Pope Pius X’s encyclical Pascendi Dominici Gregis was the institution’s own diagnostic manual for the disease. Pius X called modernism “the synthesis of all heresies.” He warned that its carriers would be found inside the Church, fluent in her language, in possession of her offices, using all of it to dismantle her doctrine without ever appearing to do so.

He wrote that warning 119 years ago. He could not have known it would describe a future Pope so precisely.

The signs Pius X listed as the marks of the modernist are now public papal teaching. Doctrine evolves with the consciousness of the age. Religion is reduced to “shared spiritual experience.” The exclusive truth claims of revelation are softened into “rays of truth” reflected in every system. The moral law is subordinated to pastoral sentiment. The hierarchy speaks the language of dialogue while the deposit is hollowed out from within.

Pius X had a name for the man who keeps the office, wears the vestments, recites the Creed at Mass, and in his public teaching erases the very content the Creed exists to preserve.

He did not call him Catholic.

V. Where to Stand
There are Catholics reading this who are theologically grieving. I have heard from many of you. The grief is not a sign that your faith is failing. It is a sign that your faith is alive enough to recognize a contradiction when you see one.

The temptation in this moment is to find another institutional refuge. The SSPX. The sedevacantists. The benevacantists. The traditionalist bloggers who promise that the “real” Pope is somewhere else and that the answer to the crisis is simply to find an unbroken line of legitimate magisterium and submit to it.

That temptation is also a trap. The crisis the Catholic Church faces was caused by trusting the institution itself as the final arbiter of doctrine. Getting a different man into the chair of Peter does not solve a problem that runs deeper than any single occupant.

The order in the Bible is Christ first, the Church second. Find the Lord, and you will find His people. Acts 17:11... “These were more fair-minded than those in Thessalonica, in that they received the word with all readiness, and searched the Scriptures daily to find out whether these things were so.”

The Bereans checked an Apostle against the Scriptures. The text says Paul commended them for it. The pattern is established. The standard is the Word. The pew-sitter is permitted... commanded, actually... to test even the Apostle against the text.

If Paul could be tested, the Pope can be tested. The man in the white cassock is not above what Paul himself submitted to. If he refuses to be measured against the Word, that refusal is itself the measurement.

Read the Book. Not as an institution’s possession but as God’s voice. The Bible was not given to a magisterium. It was given to a people. Every page of the Old and New Testaments was written to be read by believers, weighed by believers, obeyed by believers. The Bereans had no Pope. They had Moses and the Prophets. That was enough.

If you are a Catholic in agony over what your Pope is doing, the call is to find Christ Himself in the only place He has guaranteed to be found... in His Word, in His Spirit, and in the company of those who hold to both.

Revelation 18:4 stands at the end of the Bible for a reason... “Come out of her, my people, lest you share in her sins, and lest you receive of her plagues.”

The verse is a warning to people inside an institution that has gone wrong. It assumes God has people inside that institution. It calls them out. The call is gentle in form and absolute in content. Come out. Stop sharing the sins. Stop sharing the consequences.

So, is the Pope Catholic?

By the standard of his own institution... the evidence on the public record says no.

By the standard of Scripture... the evidence on the public record says no.

By the standard of the world that applauds him, claps for him, invites him to address parliaments, defends him in the press, and celebrates him at the United Nations... yes. By that standard, he is something better than Catholic. He is everything the world has always wanted the Catholic Church to become.

Luke 6:26... “Woe to you when all men speak well of you, for so did their fathers to the false prophets.”

The applause is the answer.
