The Shadow Economy Nobody Talked About

For years... many years... China had been building something that almost nobody in the media understood or bothered to explain. Not a traditional alliance. Not a military pact. Something more dangerous. A parallel economic system designed to operate entirely outside the reach of the United States dollar.

Here is how it worked. Iran was shipping China roughly 1.5 million barrels of oil per day... nearly all of Iran’s crude exports ended up in Chinese ports, accounting for about thirteen percent of China’s total seaborne crude intake. Forget normal channels. This was a shadow fleet of tankers, ship-to-ship transfers at sea, and a network of small independent Chinese refineries that operate below the radar of state-owned enterprises. The entire system was designed to be invisible to sanctions enforcement and invisible to the dollar-based financial system. And every barrel was settled in Chinese yuan. Not dollars. Yuan.

Venezuela was running the same playbook on this side of the world. Under Maduro, Chinese companies had invested billions in Venezuelan oil, mining, and surveillance infrastructure. Huawei and ZTE built the telecom networks. ZTE developed the “Fatherland Card”... a system Maduro used to track voting patterns, ration food, and monitor the population. A Chinese digital police state, exported to Latin America. And the crude was flowing to Chinese refineries through the same shadow fleet, the same sanctions-evasion routes, settled in the same currency.

Russia was the third pipeline. Discounted Russian crude flowing to China while Moscow spent everything it had on Ukraine. Three countries. Three sanctioned oil pipelines. One currency. One strategy: kill the dollar as the world’s reserve currency and replace it with a yuan-based settlement system anchored by the very regimes the United States was trying to isolate.

That was the real game. Not barrels of oil. The death of the dollar. And nobody was stopping it. Until January 3, 2026.

Venezuela: Severing the Western Artery

You know the raid. You know Maduro is in federal custody. What you need to understand is what happened the morning after... because that is where the great power strategy begins.

Within days, the United States took control of Venezuela’s oil sales. Not symbolic control. Operational control. All proceeds flowing into U.S.-managed accounts held in Qatar. Within two weeks, the first sale closed at five hundred million dollars. By the end of February, the total was approaching two billion dollars... forty million barrels moved at roughly fifty dollars a barrel. Vitol and Trafigura handling the trades. American energy companies lining up to invest. Chevron already operating. Shell expressing interest. A general license from the Treasury Department’s Office of Foreign Assets Control authorizing U.S. companies to buy, sell, transport, store, and refine Venezuelan crude.

But here is the line in that license that changes everything. The line nobody in the media highlighted. Transactions involving sanctioned vessels or entities connected to China, Russia, North Korea, Iran, or Cuba remain strictly prohibited. Read that again. You’re looking at an artery being severed. Chinese independent refineries that had been snapping up sanctioned Venezuelan crude through back channels for years... overnight, cut out of the network. They can still buy Venezuelan oil, sure... but only on the open market, at fair market prices, settled in dollars, through legitimate channels monitored by the United States. The shadow pipeline that anchored yuan settlement in the Western Hemisphere is dead.

And it’s not just oil. If it were just oil, this would have happened during the Guaidó crisis in 2019. What changed is minerals. Venezuela’s Orinoco Mining Arc... the southern Guayana Shield region... contains coltan, rare earths, bauxite, lithium, tin, gold. The raw materials the twenty-first-century economy runs on. The raw materials China currently controls ninety percent of global processing for. Ninety percent. Think of that number. And now those deposits sit in a country whose interim government is cooperating with Washington, whose mining laws are being reformed to welcome Western investment, and whose OFAC licenses explicitly prohibit Chinese and Russian entities from participating. In February, the U.S. launched Project Vault... a twelve-billion-dollar critical minerals stockpile designed to break Beijing’s chokehold on supply chains. On March 5, Acting President Rodríguez announced the mining law reform is underway. The International Crisis Group confirmed the administration is invested in both securing raw materials for domestic use and blocking Chinese access to Venezuelan minerals. The pieces are moving fast.

One more thing about Venezuela, and this one echoes across every arms bazaar on the planet. Russia had spent billions equipping Maduro’s military. Su-30 fighters. S-300 air defense systems. Buk-M2E medium-range missiles. Iranian drones. Chinese radar and surveillance equipment. And when two hundred American special operators went into downtown Caracas, all of that equipment... every system, every platform, every piece of hardware Moscow and Beijing had sold as world-class... folded in hours. The S-300s never fired. Photographs showed destroyed Buk-M2E launchers scattered across the country. Secretary of War Hegseth put it simply: “Seems those Russian air defenses didn’t quite work so well, did they?” That Venezuelan episode sent a message far beyond Venezuela. Governments weighing arms deals with Moscow or Beijing watched that night and began rethinking the cost. We’ll come back to that.

Cuba: The Forward Base Goes Dark

Phase 1 explained how Cuba is being strangled without a shot fired... the oil blockade, Mexico suspending shipments, the executive order threatening tariffs on any country that supplies fuel. All true. But Phase 1 told the domestic story. The humanitarian collapse. The lights going out. Here is the geopolitical story... the one that connects Cuba to the destruction of the CRINK network.

Cuba was not just a communist holdout. Cuba was an operational forward base for every adversary the United States has. Russian military intelligence had personnel on the ground. An estimated one hundred and twenty Russian troops stationed on the island. Russian and Chinese electronic warfare systems were creating what military analysts called a “contested electromagnetic spectrum” in the Caribbean... meaning they could degrade American communications and surveillance in our own backyard. Iran’s Revolutionary Guard and Hezbollah had operational connections running through Cuba and Venezuela. Iran reportedly maintained a drone production facility in Venezuelan territory capable of producing drones that could reach the United States. And Cuban intelligence services were embedded so deeply in Maduro’s security apparatus that thirty-two Cuban soldiers died defending his fortress the night we came for him.

That entire network... Russian troops, Chinese electronic warfare, Iranian proxy connections, Cuban intelligence coordination... is collapsing. Not because we bombed it. Because we cut the fuel. Cuba’s electrical deficit has hit 1,800 megawatts. The power grid is functionally dead. Ninety-six percent of small and medium businesses are at risk. Garbage is rotting in the streets of Havana because the collection trucks have no diesel. Schools suspended. Hospitals on generators that are running dry. And the countries that were supposed to sustain this forward base? Nowhere. Russia promised it would never abandon Cuba. Putin met with Cuba’s foreign minister on February 18 and pledged support. Words. Just words. Moscow couldn’t get a single tanker through. The U.S. seized a Russian-flagged ship in the North Atlantic heading for Cuba. Russia had to evacuate five thousand of its own tourists from the island because there was no jet fuel to fly them home. Think of that. Russia can’t protect its own vacationers. Let alone a strategic ally.

China offered sympathy. Canada sent food aid. Mexico sent humanitarian supplies from Veracruz. But nobody... nobody... sent oil. Because the executive order made it clear: supply Cuba, pay the tariff. And nobody is willing to pay that price. The last forward operating base for Russian, Chinese, and Iranian influence in the Western Hemisphere is going dark. Every intelligence operative, every surveillance system, every proxy connection on that island is losing power... literally and strategically... simultaneously.

Iran: The Keystone of the Anti-American Architecture

Phase 1 covered the military operation... Khamenei killed, the regime decapitated, the nuclear program set back a generation. What Phase 1 did not cover is why Iran’s destruction matters far beyond the Middle East. Because Iran was not just a regional menace. Iran was the keystone of the entire anti-American economic and military architecture that China and Russia had spent two decades building.

Five days ago, The Diplomat... not a conservative outlet, not a pro-Trump publication... ran a piece calling the strikes on Iran a “catastrophic geoeconomic earthquake” for China. Their analysis was devastating. Iran was the final pillar in what they described as a “triple shock” to Beijing’s clandestine energy network. Pillar one: Venezuelan crude seized by Washington in January. Pillar two: Russian oil output degraded by Ukrainian drone strikes on energy infrastructure. Pillar three: Iranian supply choked off by the destruction of the regime. Three pillars. All gone in sixty days. And the pain for Beijing goes far beyond paying more per barrel.

Iran was the eastern anchor of China’s Belt and Road Initiative... the land-based trade corridor Beijing designed to bypass the maritime chokepoints the U.S. Navy controls. The International North-South Transport Corridor, connecting Russia to Indian Ocean markets through Iran’s strategic port at Bandar Abbas, had just hit record traffic in January 2026. First regular container trains linking Moscow to the Iranian coast. The U.S. and Israel hit Bandar Abbas in the February 28 strikes. That corridor is now in jeopardy. China’s entire strategy to escape the “Malacca Dilemma”... its dependency on sea lanes America can shut down... ran through Iranian territory. That strategy just hit a wall.

Iran was also a massive customer for Russian and Chinese arms. A twenty-year strategic partnership with Moscow. A twenty-five-year cooperation program with Beijing. Su-35 fighters on order. S-400 air defense systems deployed. Chinese drone components, missile propellant precursors, BeiDou satellite navigation systems integrated into Iranian military operations. Every system, every platform, every piece of technology... destroyed or rendered irrelevant by American and Israeli air power. This is the second time in three months that Russian and Chinese defense equipment has been publicly annihilated... first in Caracas, now in Tehran. That is a pattern. And every arms customer in Africa, South Asia, Southeast Asia, and Latin America is seeing it. The marketing brochure for Russian and Chinese weapons just became a casualty report.

And then there is the yuan. Iran’s entire oil trade with China was settled in yuan through a closed-loop system designed to circumvent the dollar. With that system destroyed, and the Venezuelan pipeline under American control, and Russian capacity degraded... the “sanctioned triumvirate” that anchored Beijing’s campaign to internationalize the yuan and unseat dollar hegemony is shattered. Beijing must now return to global spot markets. Pay war-inflated premiums. Settle in dollars. The crusade to kill the dollar just got set back a decade. Maybe more.

China: The Real Target

Let me say it plainly, because nobody else will. The real target of everything that has happened in the last sixty-seven days... every raid, every strike, every blockade, every OFAC license, every seized tanker... is China.

Not China the country. Not the Chinese people. China the strategy. The strategy that Beijing has been executing for twenty years to replace the United States as the dominant power on earth. Not through war. Through economics. Through supply chain control. Through energy dependence. Through currency manipulation. Through the patient, methodical construction of a parallel world order where America doesn’t matter.

That strategy had four pillars, and the President just knocked out three of them.

Pillar one was energy. China was buying sanctioned oil from Iran, Venezuela, and Russia at steep discounts, settling in yuan, building an alternative financial system that didn’t need the dollar. That network is destroyed. Venezuelan oil now flows through U.S.-controlled accounts. Iranian supply is in chaos. Russia is selling whatever it can to fund a war it’s losing. Beijing’s access to cheap, off-the-books crude has evaporated, and with it the foundation of the yuan-settlement architecture.

Pillar two was critical minerals. China controls roughly ninety percent of global rare earth processing and over two-thirds of critical mineral refining. That dominance is the single greatest strategic vulnerability the United States faces... greater than any military threat, because without those minerals you cannot build the weapons, the chips, the batteries, or the infrastructure that power runs on. Venezuela’s Orinoco Mining Arc, now opening to American investment under reformed mining laws, represents the most significant potential break in that monopoly in the Western Hemisphere. The administration launched a twelve-billion-dollar critical minerals stockpile in February. A fifty-four-country rare earths summit was convened on February 4. A $1.6 billion investment in USA Rare Earth was announced in January. You’re looking at the foundations of a supply chain that doesn’t run through Beijing. First time. Maybe ever.

Pillar three was the Belt and Road... China’s trillion-dollar project to build trade infrastructure across Eurasia, the Middle East, Africa, and Latin America, embedding Chinese technology, Chinese standards, and Chinese political influence into the economies of the developing world. Iran was the critical Middle Eastern node... the land bridge that bypassed American sea power. Venezuela was the Latin American anchor... Chinese companies embedded in oil, mining, telecom, and surveillance. Cuba was the Caribbean listening post. Syria, which fell in December 2024 with the collapse of Assad, was the Levant corridor. Every one of those nodes is now either destroyed, under American influence, or in a state of collapse. The Belt and Road didn’t just lose a few projects. It lost the strategic logic that made it a threat.

Pillar four is the one still standing: the military. China’s navy, its missile forces, its space capabilities, its cyber operations. Those remain intact and those remain dangerous. Nobody is pretending otherwise. But a military without the economic architecture to sustain it... without cheap energy, without captive mineral supply chains, without a currency strategy, without a network of client states providing forward basing and intelligence... is a military that can defend China’s borders but cannot project power globally. And that is exactly where Beijing now finds itself.

CNN reported four days ago that the U.S. had taken out two of China’s closest allies in two months and Beijing had done “very little about it.” The piece quoted a political scientist from the National University of Singapore: “Iran has been a longtime partner of the PRC, but it is far away and not existential or perhaps even critical to the PRC.” That is a polite way of saying China did the math and decided Iran wasn’t worth a confrontation with Washington. And that calculation... that moment of restraint, of pragmatism, of choosing the upcoming summit with Trump over solidarity with a dying regime... tells you everything about where the power actually lies. Beijing blinked. Twice. Once for Maduro. Once for Khamenei. And every country in the world that was being courted by Chinese investment, Chinese loans, Chinese weapons, and Chinese promises of a multipolar future watched Beijing blink and understood what it meant.

It meant the alternative doesn’t work. It meant the insurance doesn’t pay. It meant that when the United States decides to act, China will issue a statement, make a phone call, and go back to planning its next trade summit. A business relationship with no warranty... And every nation that was hedging between Washington and Beijing just learned what that hedge is worth.

North Korea: The Last Man Standing

Which leaves the fourth member of CRINK. The one nobody came for. The one that is, in some ways, the most revealing.

North Korea’s response to the destruction of Iran was a statement from an unnamed Foreign Ministry spokesperson. Pro forma. Dutiful. Empty. No threats. No military posturing. No movement. Just a quiet registration that something catastrophic had happened to a partner, and a careful calculation about what it means for Pyongyang.

What it means is isolation. Iran was a major supplier of drones, missiles, and ammunition to Russia for the Ukraine war. The Shahed series. All of that production capacity is now either destroyed or being consumed by Iran’s own desperate defense needs. Which means Russia’s demand for North Korean military hardware... ammunition, short-range ballistic missiles, labor... just spiked. Pyongyang fills the gap. Pyongyang gets leverage over Moscow. But Pyongyang also loses the network. The web of technology transfer, sanctions evasion, weapons know-how, and diplomatic cover that Iran anchored on the eastern end and Venezuela anchored on the western end... that web is torn apart. North Korea is more useful to Russia than ever, but more alone than it has been since the Cold War.

Kim Jong Un responded by testing cruise missiles from a brand-new destroyer and accelerating uranium enrichment at Yongbyon and Kangson. That is the reaction of a man who just watched two of his partners get destroyed and concluded his only safety is a bigger arsenal. That is a problem. But it is a problem faced by an increasingly cornered regime with no functioning alliance network, no sanctions-evasion pipeline, no diplomatic cover from Beijing, and a sole patron in Moscow that is bleeding out in Ukraine.

Why the Donroe Doctrine Is Now Permanent

In Phase 1, I used the metaphor of a house with a flooded basement, a collapsing roof, and a gas leak. You have to fix the emergencies before you can be the low-maintenance homeowner you promised to be. That metaphor was about the military operations. Phase 2 is about what you find when you rip the walls open... and it turns out the previous owners didn’t just let the pipes burst. They let someone else wire the whole house to a different grid. A grid controlled from Beijing, running through Moscow, with junction boxes in Tehran, Caracas, and Havana.

That grid is now destroyed. And that is why the Donroe Doctrine is no longer a campaign promise. It is a structural reality. Let me explain why it holds.

The doctrine says: no permanent deployments, no nation-building, no forever wars, no exporting democracy at gunpoint. Get in, fix the emergency, arrange cooperative locals, get out. And the critics say: that never works, because the conditions that created the threat just regenerate and you end up going back. Iraq proved it. Afghanistan proved it. Libya proved it.

But Iraq, Afghanistan, and Libya had something that the current situation does not. They had a functioning external support network ready to fill the vacuum. ISIS was funded by Gulf money and staffed by former Baathist officers connected to regional intelligence services. The Taliban was sustained by Pakistani safe havens and Gulf financing. Libyan militias were armed by competing regional powers. The vacuum was never empty because there was always someone ready to fill it.

Who fills the vacuum now? In Venezuela... who arms the insurgency? Russia, whose weapons were just publicly humiliated and whose military is consumed by Ukraine? China, which has never projected military power into the Western Hemisphere and just watched two allies fall without lifting a finger? Iran, whose regime is headless and whose Revolutionary Guard is fighting for its own survival? Cuba, which can’t keep the lights on? There is nobody. The interim government has an oil revenue stream controlled by Washington. American companies are investing. The military is cooperating. There is no external patron waiting to destabilize it, because every external patron has been neutralized.

In Cuba... who sustains the regime? Venezuela was the lifeline. Venezuela is now an American partner. Russia can’t deliver oil. China won’t risk American tariffs. The regime has no external support structure. It either makes a deal or it collapses under its own weight. Either way, the forward base goes dark permanently.

In Iran... the situation is more complex and more dangerous, and I won’t pretend otherwise. There is active combat. Americans are dying. The regime’s remnants are fighting. But the leadership is gone. The nuclear infrastructure is destroyed. The proxy network... Hamas degraded, Hezbollah degraded, the Houthis isolated, the Assad corridor already collapsed in December 2024... is in tatters. And the external patrons? Russia issued a condemnation and went back to Ukraine. China issued a condemnation and started planning a trade summit. North Korea issued a condemnation and tested a missile. Nobody is coming. Nobody is rebuilding this regime.

That is the difference. That is why this is not Iraq. The previous interventions failed because they knocked down regimes while the support networks that sustained them remained intact. This time, the support network was the target. Venezuela... everybody said it was about Maduro. It was never about Maduro. It was about cutting the western anchor of Chinese energy penetration, Russian arms sales, and Iranian proxy infrastructure in the entire hemisphere. Cuba... same thing. Forget the communism story. Well... not entirely, but more importantly.... that island was a forward operating base for Russian intelligence, Chinese electronic warfare, Iranian operational connectivity... and we shut it down. And Iran... people think it was about the bomb. The bomb was part of it, a big part, but the real target was the eastern anchor of Belt and Road, the yuan settlement network, and the weapons-export credibility of Moscow and Beijing. All of it. Simultaneously.

President Trump dismantled the system that created them. And a system that has been dismantled does not regenerate in two years or five years or ten years. It takes decades to rebuild what was just destroyed... the financial networks, the trust relationships, the arms pipelines, the diplomatic cover, the currency architecture, the intelligence sharing, the forward basing agreements. Decades. And in those decades, the Donroe Doctrine holds. Not because of American restraint. Not because of good intentions. Because the conditions that required intervention no longer exist. The emergency is over. The pipes are fixed. The house is clean.

And now we maintain it.
